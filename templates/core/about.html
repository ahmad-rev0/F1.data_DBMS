About this build

This project is a teaching scaffold for COMP3013 Database Management Systems: reference tables are defined in database/schema.sql, indexed in database/indexes.sql, and populated with scripts/load_f1_csv.py from the CSV bundle in CSV files/ (Kaggle: Rohan Rao, Formula 1 World Championship).

Django manages authentication tables plus accounts_profile and accounts_driver_follow; all championship tables are unmanaged ORM models mapped to the hand-authored MySQL schema so your ER diagram and DDL stay first-class deliverables.

Two high-volume physical tables meet the “>50,000 rows in at least two tables” rubric: lap_time (raw timing telemetry) and lap_reporting_fact (denormalised copy with season, venue, and driver labels for analytics queries).

SQL extras: database/procedures.sql (callable from the “SQL procedure” standings link), database/triggers.sql (non-negative race_result.points), and scripts/benchmark_performance.py for insert/search/delete timings.

Django management command: python manage.py load_f1_data (same as the CSV script). Staff can reload data or edit race points from the Admin panel.
